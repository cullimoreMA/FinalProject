Honda: A Legacy of Innovation and Excellence

Honda, founded in 1948 by Soichiro Honda and Takeo Fujisawa, began as a motorcycle company and quickly became the worlds largest motorcycle manufacturer by 1959. Expanding into automobiles, Honda introduced iconic models like the Civic, Accord, and NSX, renowned for their reliability, performance, and innovative technology.


Honda has also led advancements in environmental technology, introducing the first hybrid car in the U.S. (Honda Insight, 1999) and pioneering hydrogen fuel cell and electric vehicles. From the racetrack to the road, Honda continues to inspire innovation and trust worldwide.
